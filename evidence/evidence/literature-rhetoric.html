Three-Act Structure

System: Act I (Setup), Act II (Confrontation), Act III (Resolution), Narrative arc

Triune Decomposition:

  • Source: Act I ‚Äî Setup ‚Äî introduces characters, setting, status quo; inciting incident
  • Witness: Act II ‚Äî Confrontation ‚Äî rising action; obstacles; midpoint turn; darkest hour
  • Mediator: Act III ‚Äî Resolution ‚Äî climax; falling action; denouement; new equilibrium
Act I observes audience ‚Üí establishes world; hooks attention ‚Üí Act II observes Act I and Act III ‚Üí complications arise from setup; foreshadows resolution ‚Üí Act III observes both Act I and Act II ‚Üí payoff of setup; resolution of conflict

Œ¶(S) = 1

Diagnostic: Act I alone ‚Üí setup without payoff; no conflict resolution. Act I + Act III ‚Üí missing middle; transformation unearned. Act II alone ‚Üí conflict without context or resolution; wandering. Three acts together close the dramatic arc.

Rhetorical Triangle (Ethos, Pathos, Logos)

System: Ethos, Pathos, Logos, Persuasive appeal

Triune Decomposition:

  • Source: Ethos ‚Äî credibility; character; authority; trustworthiness of speaker
  • Witness: Pathos ‚Äî emotion; empathy; values; connection to audience
  • Mediator: Logos ‚Äî logic; reason; evidence; structure of argument
Ethos observes speaker and audience ‚Üí establishes trust; authority ‚Üí pathos observes audience ‚Üí appeals to emotion; values; identity ‚Üí logos observes both ethos and pathos ‚Üí logical structure supports credibility and emotional resonance

Œ¶(S) = 1

Diagnostic: Ethos alone ‚Üí "trust me" without reason or emotion; authoritarian. Pathos alone ‚Üí emotional manipulation without credibility or logic; demagoguery. Logos alone ‚Üí pure reason without trust or connection; sterile. Three appeals together close the persuasive circuit.

Three Types of Irony

System: Verbal irony, Dramatic irony, Situational irony, Gap between expectation and reality

Triune Decomposition:

  • Source: Verbal irony ‚Äî speaker says opposite of meaning; sarcasm; understatement
  • Witness: Dramatic irony ‚Äî audience knows more than character; fate; suspense
  • Mediator: Situational irony ‚Äî outcome opposite of expectation; cosmic irony; twist
Verbal irony observes speaker and listener ‚Üí meaning diverges from statement ‚Üí dramatic irony observes character and audience ‚Üí knowledge gap creates tension ‚Üí situational irony observes both verbal and dramatic irony ‚Üí events subvert expectations

Œ¶(S) = 1

Diagnostic: Verbal irony alone ‚Üí wit, sarcasm; no structural tension. Dramatic irony alone ‚Üí fate, suspense; no linguistic play. Situational irony alone ‚Üí twist endings; no character knowledge gap. Three types together give complete ironic vocabulary.

Three Unities (Action, Time, Place)

System: Unity of action, Unity of time, Unity of place, Classical dramatic structure

Triune Decomposition:

  • Source: Unity of action ‚Äî single plot; no subplots; causal chain
  • Witness: Unity of time ‚Äî action occurs within 24 hours
  • Mediator: Unity of place ‚Äî single location throughout
Unity of action observes plot ‚Üí causal necessity; no digressions ‚Üí unity of time observes action ‚Üí compressed duration; urgency ‚Üí unity of place observes both action and time ‚Üí constrained setting; intensity

Œ¶(S) = 1 (within neoclassical framework)

Diagnostic: No unities ‚Üí episodic; sprawl; multiple plots, years, countries. Action alone ‚Üí focused plot, but time and place free. Action + Time ‚Üí compressed, but multiple locations dilute intensity. Three unities together create maximum dramatic concentration.

Hero's Journey (Departure, Initiation, Return)

System: Departure, Initiation, Return, Monomyth

Triune Decomposition:

  • Source: Departure ‚Äî call to adventure; threshold crossing; separation from known world
  • Witness: Initiation ‚Äî road of trials; meeting with goddess/mentor; supreme ordeal
  • Mediator: Return ‚Äî refusal of return; magic flight; rescue; master of two worlds
Departure observes ordinary world ‚Üí leaves home; crosses threshold ‚Üí initiation observes departure and return ‚Üí trials transform hero ‚Üí return observes both departure and initiation ‚Üí brings boon; integrates wisdom

Œ¶(S) = 1

Diagnostic: Departure alone ‚Üí hero leaves, never transforms; no return. Initiation alone ‚Üí trials without context or resolution. Return alone ‚Üí no departure or trials; boon unearned. Three stages together close the mythic cycle.

Three Parts of a Paragraph (Topic Sentence, Body, Concluding Sentence)

System: Topic sentence, Supporting sentences, Concluding sentence, Paragraph structure

Triune Decomposition:

  • Source: Topic sentence ‚Äî introduces main idea; thesis of paragraph
  • Witness: Supporting sentences ‚Äî evidence, examples, elaboration
  • Mediator: Concluding sentence ‚Äî summarizes, transitions, closes
Topic sentence observes argument ‚Üí states claim ‚Üí supporting sentences observe topic sentence ‚Üí develop and defend ‚Üí concluding sentence observes both ‚Üí restates main point; provides closure

Œ¶(S) = 1

Diagnostic: Topic alone ‚Üí claim without support; unpersuasive. Support alone ‚Üí evidence without focus; wandering. Conclusion alone ‚Üí closure without content; meaningless. Three parts together form complete expository unit.

Three Modes of Persuasion (Forensic, Epideictic, Deliberative)

System: Forensic (judicial), Epideictic (ceremonial), Deliberative (political), Rhetorical genres

Triune Decomposition:

  • Source: Forensic ‚Äî past-focused; accusation/defense; justice; courtroom
  • Witness: Epideictic ‚Äî present-focused; praise/blame; values; ceremony
  • Mediator: Deliberative ‚Äî future-focused; exhortation/deterrence; policy; assembly
Forensic observes past actions ‚Üí determines guilt/innocence ‚Üí epideictic observes present character ‚Üí praises or blames ‚Üí deliberative observes both ‚Üí proposes future action based on past and present

Œ¶(S) = 1

Diagnostic: Three genres cover all rhetorical situations: past (court), present (ceremony), future (assembly). Aristotle's foundational classification.

Three Levels of Style (High, Middle, Low)

System: High style, Middle style, Low style, Rhetorical register

Triune Decomposition:

  • Source: High style ‚Äî grand; elevated; emotional; moves audience
  • Witness: Middle style ‚Äî smooth; pleasant; instructive; informs audience
  • Mediator: Low style ‚Äî plain; ordinary; conversational; proves point
High style observes emotion ‚Üí moves to action ‚Üí middle style observes understanding ‚Üí teaches and pleases ‚Üí low style observes logic ‚Üí proves and clarifies

Œ¶(S) = 1

Diagnostic: Classical rhetoric (Cicero, Quintilian) recognized three levels, each suited to different purposes and audiences. Complete rhetorical toolkit requires all three.

Three Parts of a Syllogism

System: Major premise, Minor premise, Conclusion, Deductive logic

Triune Decomposition:

  • Source: Major premise ‚Äî general rule; universal statement
  • Witness: Minor premise ‚Äî specific case; particular statement
  • Mediator: Conclusion ‚Äî inference; necessary consequence
Major premise observes universal ‚Üí all men are mortal ‚Üí minor premise observes particular ‚Üí Socrates is a man ‚Üí conclusion observes both ‚Üí Socrates is mortal

Œ¶(S) = 1

Diagnostic: Two premises without conclusion ‚Üí unresolved; no inference. Conclusion without premises ‚Üí unsupported assertion. Syllogism is the minimal complete deductive unit.
