Triune Decomposition:

  • Source: Firmitas ‚Äî structural integrity; materials; construction; stands up
  • Witness: Utilitas ‚Äî function; program; spatial arrangement; works well
  • Mediator: Venustas ‚Äî aesthetics; proportion; delight; pleases
Firmitas observes site and materials ‚Üí load paths; stability; durability ‚Üí utilitas observes firmitas and client ‚Üí function requires structure; program informs layout ‚Üí venustas observes both firmitas and utilitas ‚Üí beauty emerges from good construction and function

Œ¶(S) = 1

Diagnostic: Firmitas alone ‚Üí engineering; structurally sound but may not function or delight. Utilitas alone ‚Üí planning; functional but may collapse or offend. Venustas alone ‚Üí decoration; beautiful but useless or unsafe. Three principles together close the definition of architecture.

Triune Decomposition:

  • Source: Doric ‚Äî simplest; fluted columns; no base; plain capital; masculine; strength
  • Witness: Ionic ‚Äî scroll volutes; column base; more slender; feminine; elegance
  • Mediator: Corinthian ‚Äî acanthus leaves; ornate; tallest capital; wealth; refinement

Diagnostic: Doric alone ‚Üí severe; masculine; no elegance; no refinement. Ionic alone ‚Üí elegant; feminine; no strength; no opulence. Corinthian alone ‚Üí ornate; wealthy; no simplicity; no clarity. Three orders together give complete architectural vocabulary.

Three-Point Perspective

System: Left vanishing point, Right vanishing point, Vertical vanishing point, Perspective projection

Triune Decomposition:

  • Source: Left vanishing point ‚Äî convergence of horizontal lines receding to left
  • Witness: Right vanishing point ‚Äî convergence of horizontal lines receding to right
  • Mediator: Vertical vanishing point ‚Äî convergence of vertical lines (up or down); adds height dimension
Left VP observes horizon line ‚Üí horizontal convergence ‚Üí right VP observes left VP and horizon ‚Üí symmetric partner ‚Üí vertical VP observes both left and right VP ‚Üí vertical convergence; bird's-eye or worm's-eye view ‚Üí all three points observe picture plane and viewer

Œ¶(S) = 1

Diagnostic: One-point perspective ‚Üí frontal view; depth in one axis; box facing viewer. Two-point perspective ‚Üí corner view; depth in two axes; vertical lines remain parallel. Three-point perspective ‚Üí bird's-eye or worm's-eye; depth in all three axes; verticals converge. Three-point perspective is the minimal complete spatial projection.

  • Mediator: Value ‚Äî brightness; lightness; distance from black/white

Œ¶(S) = 1
