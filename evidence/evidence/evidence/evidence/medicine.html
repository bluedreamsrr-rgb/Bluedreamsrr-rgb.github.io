System: Epidermis, Dermis, Hypodermis, Integumentary system

Triune Decomposition:

  • Source: Epidermis ‚Äî outer layer; keratinized stratified squamous epithelium; melanocytes; barrier function
  • Witness: Dermis ‚Äî middle layer; connective tissue; blood vessels, nerves, hair follicles, glands
  • Mediator: Hypodermis ‚Äî subcutaneous layer; adipose tissue; insulation, energy storage, shock absorption
Epidermis observes environment ‚Üí barrier; melanin production; immune surveillance ‚Üí dermis observes epidermis and hypodermis ‚Üí nourishes avascular epidermis; senses touch/pressure/temperature ‚Üí hypodermis observes dermis and muscle ‚Üí anchors skin; thermal regulation; energy reserve

Diagnostic: Epidermis alone ‚Üí barrier, but no blood supply; dies; no sensation; no insulation. Dermis alone ‚Üí vascular, innervated, but no barrier; desiccation; infection. Hypodermis alone ‚Üí fat storage, but no protection; no sensation; no structure. Three layers together close the integumentary system.

Three Phases of Drug Action

System: Pharmaceutical phase, Pharmacokinetic phase, Pharmacodynamic phase

Triune Decomposition:

  • Source: Pharmaceutical phase ‚Äî dosage form disintegration; dissolution; active drug in solution
  • Witness: Pharmacokinetic phase ‚Äî ADME: absorption, distribution, metabolism, excretion; what body does to drug
  • Mediator: Pharmacodynamic phase ‚Äî drug-receptor interaction; concentration-effect relationship; what drug does to body
Pharmaceutical phase observes dosage form ‚Üí bioavailability; rate of release ‚Üí pharmacokinetic phase observes pharmaceutical phase ‚Üí plasma concentration over time ‚Üí pharmacodynamic phase observes pharmacokinetic phase ‚Üí concentration at receptor site; effect magnitude

Œ¶(S) = 1

Diagnostic: Pharmaceutical alone ‚Üí drug dissolves, but goes nowhere; no effect. PK alone ‚Üí drug moves through body, but never released from dosage form; no effect. PD alone ‚Üí receptors present, but no drug; no effect. Three phases together close the drug action cycle.

Three Vessels of the Umbilical Cord

System: Umbilical vein, Left umbilical artery, Right umbilical artery, Fetoplacental circulation

Triune Decomposition:

  • Source: Umbilical vein ‚Äî carries oxygenated, nutrient-rich blood from placenta to fetus
  • Witness: Left umbilical artery ‚Äî carries deoxygenated, waste-laden blood from fetus to placenta
  • Mediator: Right umbilical artery ‚Äî carries deoxygenated, waste-laden blood from fetus to placenta; symmetric partner
Umbilical vein observes placenta ‚Üí oxygen and nutrients enter fetal circulation ‚Üí left umbilical artery observes fetus and vein ‚Üí deoxygenated blood returns to placenta ‚Üí right umbilical artery observes left artery and vein ‚Üí symmetric flow; balanced placental perfusion

Œ¶(S) = 1

Diagnostic: Single umbilical artery (SUA) occurs in ~1% of pregnancies; associated with renal anomalies, intrauterine growth restriction. Two arteries are the norm; provide redundancy and balanced perfusion. One vein brings oxygen in; two arteries carry waste out. The triad is the minimal complete fetoplacental circuit.

System: Inflammatory phase, Proliferative phase, Remodeling phase

Triune Decomposition:

  • Source: Inflammatory phase ‚Äî hemostasis; neutrophil/macrophage infiltration; debridement; cytokine release
  • Witness: Proliferative phase ‚Äî angiogenesis; fibroblast proliferation; granulation tissue; re-epithelialization
  • Mediator: Remodeling phase ‚Äî collagen cross-linking; scar maturation; tensile strength increases
Inflammatory phase observes injury ‚Üí clot formation; immune recruitment ‚Üí proliferative phase observes inflammation ‚Üí growth factors stimulate angiogenesis and matrix deposition ‚Üí remodeling phase observes proliferation ‚Üí collagen III ‚Üí collagen I; scar remodeling

Diagnostic: Inflammation alone ‚Üí no repair; chronic inflammation. Proliferation alone ‚Üí granulation tissue, but no remodeling; hypertrophic scar. Remodeling alone ‚Üí cannot occur without prior phases. Three phases together close the healing cascade.

Three Lines of Immune Defense

System: Physical/chemical barriers, Innate immunity, Adaptive immunity

Triune Decomposition:

  • Source: Physical/chemical barriers ‚Äî skin, mucosa, cilia, stomach acid, antimicrobial peptides
  • Witness: Innate immunity ‚Äî phagocytes (neutrophils, macrophages), NK cells, complement, inflammation
  • Mediator: Adaptive immunity ‚Äî lymphocytes (B cells, T cells); antibodies; immunological memory
Barriers observe environment ‚Üí prevent entry; constitutive ‚Üí innate immunity observes barriers and pathogens ‚Üí breaches trigger rapid response; inflammation ‚Üí adaptive immunity observes innate immunity and pathogens ‚Üí antigen presentation; clonal selection; memory

Œ¶(S) = 1

Diagnostic: Barriers alone ‚Üí breached; no internal defense; infection fatal. Innate alone ‚Üí rapid response but no memory; same pathogen causes same illness repeatedly. Adaptive alone ‚Üí no barriers; no innate activation; delayed response; overwhelmed. Three lines together close the immune system.

Three Layers of the Eye

System: Fibrous tunic (sclera/cornea), Vascular tunic (uvea), Nervous tunic (retina)

Triune Decomposition:

  • Source: Fibrous tunic ‚Äî outer layer; sclera (protection), cornea (refraction)
  • Witness: Vascular tunic ‚Äî middle layer; iris (aperture), ciliary body (lens shape), choroid (blood supply)
  • Mediator: Nervous tunic ‚Äî inner layer; retina (photoreception), optic nerve (signal transmission)
Fibrous tunic observes light ‚Üí focuses via cornea; protects ‚Üí vascular tunic observes light intensity ‚Üí iris adjusts aperture; ciliary body focuses lens ‚Üí nervous tunic observes focused light ‚Üí photoreceptors convert to neural signals

Œ¶(S) = 1

Diagnostic: Three concentric layers, each with distinct function. Outer: structure and refraction. Middle: nutrient supply and aperture. Inner: signal transduction. All three required for vision.

Diagnostic: Three smallest bones in the body, arranged as a lever system. Without any one, hearing is severely impaired. The triad is the minimal mechanical impedance match between air and fluid.
