Instances of triune structure in markets, production, and economic systems.

Supply, Demand, and Price

System: Supply, Demand, Market-clearing price, Market equilibrium

Triune Decomposition:

  • Source: Supply ‚Äî quantity producers are willing to sell at given price
  • Witness: Demand ‚Äî quantity consumers are willing to buy at given price
  • Mediator: Price ‚Äî signal that equilibrates both; allocates scarce resources
Supply observes price ‚Üí quantity supplied increases with price ‚Üí demand observes price ‚Üí quantity demanded decreases with price ‚Üí price observes both supply and demand ‚Üí adjusts until Qs = Qd

Œ¶(S) = 1 at equilibrium

Diagnostic: No supply ‚Üí nothing to transact; demand unmet. No demand ‚Üí no reason to produce; supply collapses. No price ‚Üí no signal; shortages and surpluses persist. Price is the mediator that closes the system.

Triple Bottom Line (People, Planet, Profit)

System: People, Planet, Profit, Sustainable business

Triune Decomposition:

  • Source: People ‚Äî social equity; labor, community, human flourishing
  • Witness: Planet ‚Äî environmental sustainability; ecological health
  • Mediator: Profit ‚Äî economic viability; resource allocation signal
People observe planet ‚Üí depend on environmental resources ‚Üí planet observes people ‚Üí impacted by human activity ‚Üí profit observes both people and planet ‚Üí measures tradeoffs; incentivizes sustainable behavior

Œ¶(S) = 0 (current state) ‚Üí 1 (normative target)

Diagnostic: People without planet ‚Üí extractive, short-term, collapses ecosystem. Planet without people ‚Üí conservation without development; deserts. Profit without people or planet ‚Üí exploitation; collapses socially and environmentally. Triple bottom line is the theorem applied to sustainability.

Factors of Production (Land, Labor, Capital)

System: Land, Labor, Capital, Production process

Triune Decomposition:

  • Source: Land ‚Äî natural resources; raw materials; location
  • Witness: Labor ‚Äî human effort; work; skills
  • Mediator: Capital ‚Äî tools, machinery, infrastructure; transforms land and labor into goods
Land observes resources ‚Üí provides raw materials ‚Üí labor observes land ‚Üí applies effort ‚Üí capital observes both land and labor ‚Üí enables production; increases output

Œ¶(S) = 1

Diagnostic: Land alone ‚Üí resources unused. Labor alone ‚Üí effort without resources or tools. Capital alone ‚Üí machines idle without materials or workers. Three factors together enable production.

Three Economic Sectors (Primary, Secondary, Tertiary)

System: Primary sector, Secondary sector, Tertiary sector, Economic development

Triune Decomposition:

  • Source: Primary sector ‚Äî extraction; agriculture, mining, fishing, forestry
  • Witness: Secondary sector ‚Äî manufacturing; processing raw materials into finished goods
  • Mediator: Tertiary sector ‚Äî services; retail, transportation, education, healthcare
Primary sector observes natural resources ‚Üí extracts raw materials ‚Üí secondary sector observes primary ‚Üí manufactures goods ‚Üí tertiary sector observes both primary and secondary ‚Üí distributes, sells, services

Œ¶(S) = 1

Diagnostic: Primary alone ‚Üí raw materials with no processing or distribution. Primary + Secondary ‚Üí goods produced but not distributed. Three sectors together form complete economy. Quaternary sector (information) is an extension.

Three Functions of Money (Medium of Exchange, Unit of Account, Store of Value)

System: Medium of exchange, Unit of account, Store of value, Monetary system

Triune Decomposition:

  • Source: Medium of exchange ‚Äî facilitates transactions; eliminates barter's double coincidence of wants
  • Witness: Unit of account ‚Äî common measure of value; pricing standard
  • Mediator: Store of value ‚Äî preserves purchasing power over time
Medium of exchange observes transactions ‚Üí enables trade ‚Üí unit of account observes prices ‚Üí standardizes value ‚Üí store of value observes both ‚Üí maintains value over time; enables saving and deferred payment

Œ¶(S) = 1

Diagnostic: Money that fails any of these three functions is imperfect. Hyperinflation destroys store of value. Barter lacks medium of exchange. Without unit of account, prices cannot be compared.

Three Types of Economic Systems (Market, Command, Mixed)

System: Market economy, Command economy, Mixed economy, Economic organization

Triune Decomposition:

  • Source: Market economy ‚Äî decentralized; prices determined by supply and demand; private ownership
  • Witness: Command economy ‚Äî centralized; government planning; state ownership
  • Mediator: Mixed economy ‚Äî combines market and command elements; public and private sectors
Market observes consumer preferences ‚Üí allocates via prices ‚Üí command observes social goals ‚Üí allocates via planning ‚Üí mixed observes both ‚Üí balances efficiency and equity

Œ¶(S) = 1 (mixed economy as stable configuration)

Diagnostic: Market alone ‚Üí efficient but can produce inequality and externalities. Command alone ‚Üí equitable in theory but inefficient. Mixed economy mediates between extremes; most real economies are mixed.

Three Components of the Business Cycle (Expansion, Peak, Contraction, Trough)

System: Expansion, Peak, Contraction, Trough, Cyclical fluctuation

Triune Decomposition:

  • Source: Expansion ‚Äî increasing output, employment, income; growth phase
  • Witness: Peak ‚Äî maximum output; turning point
  • Mediator: Contraction ‚Äî decreasing output, employment, income; recession or depression
  • Boundary: Trough ‚Äî minimum output; turning point to next expansion
Expansion observes growth ‚Üí rising indicators ‚Üí peak observes expansion ‚Üí maximum reached ‚Üí contraction observes both ‚Üí downturn begins ‚Üí trough observes contraction ‚Üí minimum reached; recovery starts

Œ¶(S) = 1

Diagnostic: The cycle has four phases, but the dynamic is triadic: expansion, turning, contraction. Trough is the boundary to the next expansion.

Three Policy Tools of Central Banks

System: Interest rates, Reserve requirements, Open market operations, Monetary policy

Triune Decomposition:

  • Source: Interest rates ‚Äî cost of borrowing; influences spending and investment
  • Witness: Reserve requirements ‚Äî fraction of deposits banks must hold; affects money creation
  • Mediator: Open market operations ‚Äî buying/selling government securities; adjusts money supply
Interest rates observe economic conditions ‚Üí cost of money ‚Üí reserve requirements observe banking system ‚Üí lending capacity ‚Üí open market operations observe both ‚Üí fine-tunes money supply

Œ¶(S) = 1

Diagnostic: Three primary tools give central banks complete control over monetary policy. Different central banks emphasize different tools, but all three are available.

Three Parts of the Balance of Payments

System: Current account, Capital account, Financial account, International transactions

Triune Decomposition:

  • Source: Current account ‚Äî trade in goods and services; income flows; unilateral transfers
  • Witness: Capital account ‚Äî capital transfers; acquisition/disposal of non-financial assets
  • Mediator: Financial account ‚Äî investment flows; direct, portfolio, other investment; reserves
Current account observes trade ‚Üí net exports ‚Üí capital account observes transfers ‚Üí non-financial assets ‚Üí financial account observes both ‚Üí balances overall; must sum to zero

Œ¶(S) = 1

Diagnostic: Current account surplus must be offset by financial account deficit (capital outflow) or reserve accumulation. The three accounts must balance to zero.
